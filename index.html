Xiaoli Liu

Ph.D. Candidate in Lyles School of Civil Engineering, Purdue University

• I have strong backgrounds in heat & mass exchanger, active building envelope, thermoelectricity, and advanced materials in buildings. I have dedicated in two projects including membrane-based liquid desiccant air dehumidification and thermoelectric building envelope, and accumulated ample experience in numerical modeling, experimental design and hands-on, and programming with LabVIEW, Modelica, MATLAB, and Python.
• As the first author, I have published three papers in peer-reviewed journals with high impact factors (4.7–12.1) and delivered presentations in ASHRAE and Herrick Conferences. I am always willing to tackle research challenges with multidisciplinary knowledge.
• I plan to gradurate in May 2022, and I am open to postdoc positions.

Research Projects

Thermoelectric building envelope

• Assembled prototype of the thermoelectric building envelope.
• Designed and developed the experimental setup and a LabVIEW project to test the customized prototype in psychrometric chambers.
• Developed a 3D heat and electric coupled TE model in MATLAB and COMSOL, and a model library of TE building envelope in Dymola using Modelica language.

Thermoelectric cement composite

• Fabricated new thermoelectric (TE) cement composites with graphite/graphene and nanoparticles via dry and wet methods.
• Characterized thermelectric performances by advanced physical property measurement system.

Membrane-based liquid desiccant air dehumidification

• Designed experiment to measure the mass transfer coefficient of different types of membranes.
• Developed 2D microstructure-based heat and mass transfer model to evaluate the performance of a membrane-based dehumidifier using finite difference method in MATLAB.

IoT smart buildings

• Developed building control strategies using microcontroller named Particle Photon and its Could and mobile platform to realize data acquisition, heater on/off control, and lighting control. [See more]

Design of a variable-speed heat pump system for the residential market

• Designed system and selected all necessary components.
• Developed the EES model and predicted system performance of a heat pump for residential buildings.
